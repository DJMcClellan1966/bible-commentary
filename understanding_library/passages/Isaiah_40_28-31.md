## Understanding Isaiah 40:28-31

This passage contains 4 verses that together form a complete thought or teaching.

**Key Themes:** That Shall They, That Things Unto, Shall That Things, That God, Hast

**The Passage:**

Isaiah 40:28: Hast thou not known? hast thou not heard? The everlasting God, Jehovah, the Creator of the ends of the earth, fainteth not, neither is weary; there is no searching of his understanding.

Isaiah 40:29: He giveth power to the faint; and to him that hath no might he increaseth strength.

Isaiah 40:30: Even the youths shall faint and be weary, and the young men shall utterly fall:

Isaiah 40:31: but they that wait for Jehovah shall renew their strength; they shall mount up with wings as eagles; they shall run, and not be weary; they shall walk, and not faint.

**Understanding the Whole:**

When we read these verses together, we see how they build on each other to reveal a deeper truth. Each verse contributes to the whole, and the whole is greater than the sum of its parts. This passage invites us not just to understand individual verses, but to see how they connect to form a complete picture of God's truth.

**How to Read This Passage:**

1. Read the passage slowly, letting each verse sink in.
2. Notice how the verses connect - what does each add to the others?
3. Look for the main idea - what is the passage as a whole saying?
4. Consider the context - how does this passage fit into the book and the Bible as a whole?
5. Apply it personally - what does this mean for your relationship with God?
